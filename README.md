Welcome to Star, a train tracking application created to make commuting easier and more accessible.

Living in a busy city, I noticed how difficult it can be for commuters to navigate a complex train system. That inspired me to build a user-friendly tool that helps people track trains in real time and better understand train lines and stations.

Using React and Leaflet, I developed an interactive map-based application that displays live train locations and useful transport information. By integrating Entur’s API, the app fetches real-time train data to provide accurate and reliable updates.

The application allows users to:

View all active trains at once or filter by a specific train line
Click on a train to zoom in for a closer view
Open a popup with additional train information
Overlay railway lines and stations using GeoJSON data
Click on stations to see their names and when they were opened for use

This project was created as part of my exam, and because it uses a live train feed, the application can sometimes feel a bit laggy. The dropdown menu may take a little time to fully load, and the map can also need a few moments before everything displays smoothly. If things seem slow at first, please be patient and avoid refreshing the page.

Some trains may appear stationary or disappear temporarily during the night. This is usually because of inactive routes, delayed live updates, or limited nighttime movement in the data feed. In most cases, they will reappear or update once the route becomes active again.

This project was developed as group work. My group partner and I worked both together and separately on different parts of the application, and later merged our code into one final solution. Because of this, the number of commits may vary between team members in the repository.

The goal of this app is not just to track trains, but to help commuters and visitors travel with more confidence and convenience.
